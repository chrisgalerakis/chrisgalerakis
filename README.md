Quantitative researcher and author with technical analysis expertise across U.S. futures markets. 10+ years in data analysis and systematic modeling, focused on the non-discretionary side of financial markets: systematic flows, options positioning, volatility regimes, CTA trend signals, and cross-sectional breadth and dispersion.

The goal across everything here is the same: understand what moves markets when nobody is making a discretionary decision. Mandates, hedging, rebalancing, and positioning unwinds leave footprints, and those footprints are measurable.

## Domains

- **Systematic flows & positioning** — estimating mechanical, mandate-driven flows from vol-targeting funds, leveraged ETFs, and CTAs. Methodologies include realized-volatility-driven leverage models for vol-control, deterministic end-of-day rebalancing models for LETFs, and trend-signal reconstruction (time-series momentum) for CTA positioning.

- **Options & dealer hedging** — modeling dealer inventory and hedging flows from listed options open interest. Work covers aggregate gamma exposure (GEX) construction, strike-level gamma profiles, implied volatility skew dynamics, and fitting and smoothing the implied volatility surface.

- **Volatility** — modeling and forecasting volatility across implied and realized measures. Methodologies include the volatility risk premium as the spread between implied and subsequent realized volatility, higher-order volatility dynamics, range-based realized volatility estimators for efficiency gains over close-to-close, and volatility regime detection through statistical clustering and thresholding.

- **Cross-asset risk** — constructing composite stress indicators from cross-asset inputs. Methodology centers on principal component analysis to decompose risk into a dominant systematic factor and residual sector-specific components, yielding a signal that separates broad market stress from localized dislocations.

- **Market structure** — quantifying the internal state of an Index through breadth measures, cross-sectional return dispersion, implied correlation, and seasonality effects. The goal is to characterize whether the index is moving on aggregate versus idiosyncratic drivers.
